The Product

What is QUANTASANA?+
QUANTASANA is a wellness system that combines four modalities — guided breathwork, precision sound frequencies, vibroacoustic vibration, and 40 Hz gamma light — into a single, sequenced protocol delivered through a handcrafted lounge chair and companion app. Every session follows a three-phase arc (Arrival → Immersion → Integration) calibrated to your nervous system's natural response curve.
What does a session actually feel like?+
Imagine settling into a zero-gravity recline. Guided breathing slows your pace to ~6 breaths per minute. Sound frequencies wrap around you through spatial audio headphones while matched vibrations pulse through four independent zones in the chair beneath you. Overhead, warm light flickers at 40 Hz — fast enough that you perceive it as a gentle shimmer, not a strobe. Twenty minutes later, everything gradually fades and you re-emerge with a clarity that's hard to describe until you've felt it.
How long is a session?+
Sessions come in three tiers: Reset (10 minutes), Session (20 minutes — the standard protocol), and Deep (30 minutes). The 20-minute session is the core product — it's long enough for the full three-phase arc but short enough for daily use.
What are the session types?+
Five types, each using the same three-phase arc but with different modality emphasis: CALM (stress & anxiety relief, extended exhale breathing), FOCUS (cognitive clarity, 40 Hz gamma entrainment), RECOVER (physical recovery, therapeutic vibration sweeps), SLEEP (pre-sleep wind-down, delta-adjacent frequencies), and RENEW (emotional processing, variable intensity waves).
Do I need the chair to use the app?+
The app works standalone for guided breathwork and sound-based sessions — no chair required. But the full QUANTASANA experience requires the chair for vibroacoustic vibration and the light canopy for 40 Hz visual entrainment. Think of the app as the conductor and the chair as the orchestra.

The Science

What is vibroacoustic therapy?+
Vibroacoustic therapy (VAT) uses low-frequency sound vibrations between 30–120 Hz delivered directly to the body through transducers embedded in the chair. These vibrations activate mechanoreceptors called Pacinian corpuscles deep in your skin and joints, triggering parasympathetic nervous system shifts. The FDA classifies VAT devices as Class I therapeutic vibrators — exempt from premarket notification. The therapy was developed by Norwegian researcher Olav Skille in the 1980s and has been studied for pain management, Parkinson's, stress reduction, and muscle relaxation.
What is 40 Hz gamma entrainment?+
When your brain receives sensory stimulation at precisely 40 Hz — through light, sound, or vibration — it synchronizes its own neural oscillations to that frequency. This is called "entrainment." MIT's Picower Institute has published extensively on this, showing that 40 Hz stimulation through light and sound activates neuroprotective responses, reduces Alzheimer's-related proteins, and preserves brain volume. Their research calls this GENUS (Gamma Entrainment Using Sensory Stimulation). A 2023 study extended this to tactile vibration, showing 40 Hz vibrotactile stimulation also reduces brain pathology in mouse models.
What is the evidence for 40 Hz in humans?+
Cognito Therapeutics (co-founded by MIT's Li-Huei Tsai) ran a Phase 2 clinical trial (OVERTURE) with 76 Alzheimer's patients using 40 Hz audiovisual stimulation. Results showed a 76% reduction in cognitive decline and 69% reduction in brain volume loss over 6 months. Their Phase 3 trial (HOPE) enrolled 670 participants — the largest medical device trial focused solely on Alzheimer's. Their device received FDA Breakthrough Device Designation. It's important to note that human trials used audiovisual delivery — human clinical evidence for vibroacoustic 40 Hz specifically is still being established.
What is HRV and why does it matter?+
Heart Rate Variability (HRV) measures the variation in time between heartbeats. Higher HRV indicates a healthier, more adaptable nervous system — your body can shift smoothly between activation and recovery. The specific metric we track is RMSSD, which directly reflects parasympathetic (vagal) tone. Research shows that slow-paced breathing at ~6 breaths per minute (resonance frequency) is the most efficient way to increase HRV, and that vibroacoustic therapy sessions measurably increase parasympathetic activity.
Is QUANTASANA clinically proven?+
QUANTASANA is science-informed, not clinically proven. Our protocol design is informed by published research in HRV biofeedback, vibroacoustic therapy, and gamma brainwave entrainment — but we haven't run our own clinical trials on our specific device. We're transparent about this distinction. We don't say "treats" or "cures" anything. We say: the body's response to controlled breathing, low-frequency vibration, and sound frequency entrainment is well-documented, and we've sequenced those mechanisms into a protocol.

The app orchestrates every session — guiding your breath timing, adjusting sound frequencies, and (when connected to the chair) calibrating vibration intensity across all four body zones. It also tracks your sessions, monitors your HRV response before and after, shows your 96-hour recovery arc, and builds personalized insights over time (best session time, optimal session type, vagal tone trends).

We integrate with Oura Ring (recommended for overnight HRV tracking), Polar H10 chest strap (recommended for real-time session-level HRV), Apple Watch, WHOOP, and Garmin devices. The app uses the Terra API to normalize data across all platforms.
